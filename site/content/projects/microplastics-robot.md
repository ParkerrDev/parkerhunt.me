## Overview

Two-year project for California Science and Engineering Fair developing a fully autonomous water filtration robot capable of detecting and removing microplastics. Designed and built as a team, with personal focus on electronics and ML algorithm.

## My Contributions

**Electronics & Systems Design**
- Architected power distribution system for multiple actuators and sensors
- Integrated motor controllers, water sensors, and vision systems
- Debugged and optimized circuit reliability through multiple iterations
- Designed custom PCB layouts for miniaturization

**Machine Learning Pipeline**
- Trained CNN model in PyTorch to classify microplastics vs. organic matter
- Preprocessed training data with NumPy and Pandas from microscopy images
- Achieved ~94% accuracy on held-out test set
- Optimized model for real-time inference on embedded hardware

**Robot Control**
- Wrote firmware integrating sensor inputs with ML predictions
- Implemented automated filtration valve control based on classifications
- Tested in saltwater and freshwater environments

## Recognition

Advanced to state-level competition and presented findings at California SEEF showcase.

Python, PyTorch, Scikit-Learn, NumPy, Pandas, Arduino, Circuit Design, Machine Learning
